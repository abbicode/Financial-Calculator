50/30/20 Rule

The 50/30/20 rule is a common budget rule to help individuals manage their spendings. It states that an individual should spend 50% of one's salary on needs, 30% on wants, and 20% on savings. For example, needs would include food, housing, transportation, etc. Wants would include things that aren't necessary such as travel expenses, shopping, entertainment, etc. Lastly, savings would include retirement plan, emergency funds, debt repayment, etc. This rule is just a base budgeting method for some people, however, depending on your income, you may need to adjust the percentages. For some, 50% may not be enough of the paycheck to cover needs. Additionally, one may have a lot of debt to pay off, so they would need to adjust the last percentage to something higher. Some argue that 70/20/10/ budget might be more attainable for the average American Citizen.
